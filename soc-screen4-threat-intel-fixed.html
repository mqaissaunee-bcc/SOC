🖥 Individual Screens (One Dashboard per Display)

Each dashboard is designed for 1920 × 1080 (Full HD) as the baseline. At this resolution, all grid layouts render fully: the three-column SIEM view, the world map with side panels, the incident timeline, and the threat intel feeds.

Responsive breakpoints exist at 1400px, 1200px, 900px, and 768px, but these are graceful fallbacks that stack content vertically. They work, but lose the "wall of data at a glance" SOC feel. Standard 22"–27" monitors at 1080p are ideal.

📺 Multi-Screen Viewer

In quad view, the viewport divides into a 2×2 grid. Each quadrant gets roughly half the viewport width and height minus the control bar.

At 1920 × 1080: Each quadrant is ~960 × 490px. Below the 1200px breakpoint, so dashboards show simplified layouts. Functional but compressed.

At 2560 × 1440: Each quadrant is ~1280 × 670px. Clears the 1200px breakpoint. Most layouts stay intact.

At 3840 × 2160 (4K): Each quadrant is ~1920 × 1040px. Full HD per quadrant — the ideal experience.

Tip: If using a 1080p display for multi-screen, use the 2-up layout (Top 2 / Bottom 2 presets) instead of quad view. Each panel gets the full viewport width.

💡 Practical Advice

Four individual stations: Standard 1080p monitors of any size (22"–27") work perfectly. Bigger is better for readability at a distance, but resolution is the key factor.

Large presentation display: Push for the highest resolution available. A 55"+ TV at 4K delivers the full experience. A 1080p projector works but text in quad view will be small from more than a few feet away.

Budget option: A single 1440p ultrawide (3440 × 1440) in 2-up mode gives each panel ~1720 × 1390px — very comfortable.

🔠 Font Scaling & Readability

Dashboards use fixed pixel font sizes (10–14px for data, monospace). These don't scale with display DPI. On a large low-resolution display (e.g. 65" 1080p TV at 10ft), text will be physically large but may appear soft. On a 27" 4K monitor up close, everything is crisp but physically small.

The sweet spot depends on the combination of resolution, physical screen size, and viewing distance. For classroom settings, a 55"–65" 4K TV viewed from 6–10 feet is the ideal range.
