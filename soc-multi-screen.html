📺 Multi-Screen Viewer (This View)

The multi-screen viewer divides your display into a 2×2 grid of iframes. Each quadrant gets half your width × half your height, so the effective resolution per dashboard is:

1080p display → ~960 × 490 per quadrant (tight — some stacking occurs)
1440p display → ~1280 × 670 per quadrant (comfortable)
4K display → ~1920 × 1030 per quadrant (optimal — each quadrant is effectively Full HD)

For presentations, a 4K display or projector is the ideal choice since each quadrant gets a full 1080p-equivalent viewport.

💡 Practical Tips

Classroom TVs (55”–65”): Use 4K resolution for maximum clarity. At 1080p, the quad view will be cramped but still functional.

Desktop Monitors (24”–32”): 1440p or higher recommended for comfortable quad viewing. At 1080p, consider using individual screens instead.

Projectors: Ensure native 1080p or higher. 4K projectors deliver the best multi-screen experience.

Fullscreen: Press F11 to enter fullscreen mode and reclaim the browser chrome space — this makes a significant difference for the quad layout.

🔤 Font Scaling & Readability

Dashboard fonts are set in pixels (11–14px base). In quad view, these appear at half their normal physical size. On a 1080p display, this can make text difficult to read.

The sweet spot depends on the combination of resolution, physical screen size, and viewing distance. For classroom settings, a 55”–65” 4K TV viewed from 6–10 feet is the ideal range.
